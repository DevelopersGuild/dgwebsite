Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I don't have a programming background. Can I join anyway?

A: Yes! There are many things that we need help with, including user interface design, artwork, and team management. Of course, any programming experience you do have will help you communicate with the team.

Q: How involved do I need to be?

A: As with joining any club, the amount of time you commit is up to you. However, the more you contribute to your group's development efforts, the more connected you will feel with your group.

Q: What projects can I build?

A: You can build websites, mobile applications, games, whatever you want! We are here to support your ambitions!

Q: I have an idea for a proejct! How can I find people to help me work on it?

A: Come to one of our meetings and ask the officers. That's the quickest way!

Q: I don't have any programming experience. Will you help me learn?

A: We won't tutor you ourselves, but we can give you many resources that other students have used to successfully learn basic programming. Also, being around other programmers more skilled than yourself will help you to grow in your own programming abilities.

Q: I don't have any ideas for my own projects. Or, I don't want to make a new project. Is that still okay?

A: Of course it is! Don't feel pressured to do something you don't want to do. You may always join an existing Guild project and meet the people in that team.

Q: How often do you meet?

A: Every week. Our room number and time change every quarter to accomodate people's changing schedules, but we generally occupy the AT building on campus. Message us or subscribe to our mailing list for more specific information each quarter.

Q: I don't have a laptop. Is that okay?

A: It depends on which project you wish to work on. Many students use the school desktop computers that run Windows for their projects.

Q: Do I have to be a De Anza College student to join?

A: Yes. We are an incorporated De Anza College club, and we may only accept local students.

Q: Is there a fee for joining?

A: No. Developers' Guild is free for all De Anza College students and will always remain that way.

Q: Am I too advanced for this club?

A: You might be more technically advanced, but what we offer are teams with a purpose.
